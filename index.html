Timeline

October 2019

  • Oct 26th: Bushfire in Gospers Mountain, NSW (New South Wales) started by a lightning bolt - becomes the epicenter of the biggest forest fire to have started from a single ignition point in Australian history with ~1.1 million acres destroyed
  • Oct 26th: A dry electrical storm starts a bushfire in Port Macquarie, NSW
  • Oct 28th: Fire near Cattai, NSW burns ~30,000 acres

    • November 2019

  • Nov 12th: Fire in Lane Cove National Park, NSW
  • Nov 20th: Fire in Yorke Peninsula, SA (South Australia), destroying at least 11 homes and about 12,000 acres of land - started from a sparking electrical transformer

    • December 2019

  • Dec 14-15: Fire spreads in Southern Highlands, NSW.
  • Dec 15: backburn (seemingly controlled fire) grows out of control in Mt. Wilson and Mt. Irvine, engulfing about 865,000 acres of land
  • Dec 26: lightning sparks a fire in the Sterling Ranges, WA (Western Australia)
  • Dec 30: 3 active fires in East Gippsland, VIC (Victoria)
  • Dec 31: 3 separate fires in NSW join to form a large fire, destroying 635,000 acres of land

    • January 2020

  • Jan 2: Fires engulf East Gippsland, VIC, destroying 2,000 acres. The Currowan fire along the NSW South Coast burns more than 635,000 acres
  • Jan 3: A fire on Kangaroo Island, SA, destroying 420,000 acres of land (a third of the island)
  • Jan 4: NSW records temps of 120 degrees Fahrenheit - making it the hottest place on Earth at the time
  • Jan 5: Fires from SE VIC spreads to Bega Valley Shire, NSW, burning more than 148,000 acres
  • Jan 6: Australian Federal Government commits $2 billion to a national bushfire recovery fund. VIC’s Premier announces that 200 homes have been lost and more than 2.9 mil acres have been destroyed
  • Jan 21: The fires are estimated to have caused the deaths of 30 people and 500 million animals and $1 billion in damages
